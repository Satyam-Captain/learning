Introduction
Purpose of the document

☞ 
This document is part of an “Initiative lifecycle”
An initiative is a new plan or action to improve something or solve a problem, the “initiative lifecycle” is the lifecycle of any evolution or transformation that has an impact on the Information System. It is sequenced in 3 main phases : ideation, maturation & execution. Production of High-Level Design Dossier [HLDD], Concept Dossier [CD] and Architecture Requirements Dossier [ARD] are mapped in the bottom the overview below:
These documents are exposed in the initiative lifecycle overview below:



This document is prepared during the Preliminary Design phase. 
It is initiated after the “Go” from the M&T Board.
Depending on criteria defined & updated by Data Integration & Analytics Committee (DIAC) & IM Architecture Committee (IMAC) these committees manage this phase and decide the steps to be done, otherwise it will be done under the responsibility of the impacted PSL/Domain.
☜

This High-Level Design Dossier (HLDD) provides the results of the Preliminary Design dedicated to 
GISEH for eAction.

It describes the transformation to be addressed and presents the executive summary of the scenario’s selection. It may refer to an associated Concept Dossier (CD) if a detailed analysis of scenarios is required to make the selection.
It also defines the scope of the associated Architecture Dossiers (ARD).


The HLDD contents six distinct parts:
Context analysis: describes the project context, the change motives and the shared overall vision of its achievement.
Business Architecture: states, from business perspective, the current situation, the aimed final situation and the targeted business increments.
Information System Architecture: analyses from existing IM/IT landscape, possible solutions to fulfill business increments.
Architecture Scenarios: enables the selection of an architecture from a short-list of solutions meeting the needs described in the previous chapters
Transition Architectures and Roadmap: defines the roadmap for the change, including transition architectures.

The High-Level Design Dossier will be an input for all Architecture Dossiers required to address GiSEH for eAction realization, if decided.

Business Requirements

This chapter is dedicated to sum-up business requirements, i.e. expectation from business point of view, and all constraints the solution to be designed has to meet.

☞
Here the scope mainly regards expected services/functions, qualitative or quantitative results, any constraints known or applicable at business level.
Part of these requirements/constraints can come from Motivations previously analyzed in this HLDD.
These requirements/constraints are to be considered as related to the needs.
It is suggested to split these items into:

Functional Requirements:
They can be expressed as: user stories, use cases (UML definition), features, or any other SMART* shape.

Non Functional Requirements:
They gather all elements of constraint under which the functional requirements are to be provided: performances (time response, volume, rate, etc.), ergonomy, safety, etc. ; or any principle deliberately adopted and reducing the scope of possible solutions like:
Drivers, principles,
Technology, product,
Regulation,
 …

(*) Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Reasonable, Traceable
These requirements have to be mapped over business architecture items (business capability, process, etc.)

In addition, it is strongly recommended, at this stage, to group requirements in homogeneous and consistent packages with respect to the business context. This will surely ease the identification of business increments later on.

AS-IS Business Architecture

The purpose of this section is to define the full scope of the existing business architecture in order to locate some of the change motives coming directly from the business context, e.g. pain points and weaknesses coming from specific items of business architecture.

As a consequence, this section also describes the arrangement and relationships between existing business activities for every business process impacted by the transformation.

Insofar as the business architecture will be the major point of entry for the design of the information system architecture, the logical capability concept is defined as a pivot point between the business layer and the information system layer.

☞
To provide a picture of the business layer, all the concepts in the scope of the business process have to be addressed. 
The following concepts are basically used in such purpose:
Business Capability
Business Process and Business Activity
Business Deliverable
Logical Capability
Application Service

This way the scope of the transformation is now seen in a systemic perspective and provides a fully relevant, comprehensive and understandable picture of the sense of the transformation.

If the initiative described in this document concerns several business capabilities, we recommend describing the architecture of each business capability in a dedicated section.

Transition Architectures and Roadmap

While previous chapters focused on what has to be changed and what is the best TO-BE solution to embody that change, this chapter is dedicated to identify the best route to operate the change, starting from AS-IS situation and reaching the TO-BE situation mentioned above.
The transition approach is as follows:

1) Business stakeholders draw up a prioritized list of business activities to be transformed.
2) IT and business stakeholders derive a prioritized list of logical capability changes.
3) IT and business stakeholders consolidate a list of work packages, grouping together changes to business activities and logical capabilities. 
4) Change management-specific work packages can also be integrated.
5) The overall roadmap is finally consolidated by grouping and sequencing work packages into "increments".

Change Management Inception

Changes induced by the transformation planned in this HLDD go with a change management plan outlined in this chapter.
Each change management activity and related deliverables should be described as a work-package, so that it can be inserted into the overall roadmap at the right time. In this way, dependencies with other work packages can be taken into account.

☞
Changes induced by the transformation planned in this HLDD have an impact on organizations and day to day life of individuals in these organizations. Some aspects of the change will obviously improve daily activities of individuals inducing positive attitudes. But for some others it will only be “changes” and they can be reluctant to change the current situation.
As a result, depending on their fears and influence regarding the transformation, they can be an obstacle to its implementation and operation, compromising the expected benefits of the change.
This can be efficiently mitigated by change management provisions to be outlined here.
